PROJECT OVERVIEW:
----------------
Not at all dissimilar to the way things seem to emerge from the blurry edges of dusty rooms in the dead of night, 
Cat_Warehouse erose from mere particles of dust. 

More specifically, the idea and theming behind it came from the abrupt entry of my cat walking into my room at 
around 1:30 am, jumping onto the desk, and proceeding to sit directly on my computer and demand attention, completely  
impervious to the blanket of darkness that the world had draped over the house. 

Embracing a semi grungy, middle of the night (maybe even beginnings of dawn) quality, Cat_Warehouse is designed for nightowls 
seeking an alternative to the clean, corporate, and capatalistic feels of traditional online game stores. 

Bringing with it the same energy of an overzealous house cat knocking things off of desks, or an aloof, wild stray patrolling
an abandoned concrete jungle, Cat_Warehouse invites you to stay up late and click through dusty shelves of pixels. It urges you to find something you 
were never looking for, but now can not imagine parting without, daring you to just try and look the other way. 

DESIGN CHOICES:
--------------
Overall Style & Theme:
As a whole, I wanted the website to enbrace a "grungy but clean" aesthetic - abandoned 
warehouse with CRT TVs, floating dust particles, and an aloof cat who walks around acting as though they own the 
place (they do). The atmosphere is reminiscent of the calm and cool air that comes with it being the dead of night: unsettling but cozy, knowing  
your supposed to be in slumber but not willing to put the game down. 

Colour Palette (colours taken via the eye dropper tool directly from a picture of said problematic and attention seeking cat)
https://imgur.com/a/zhqVcLk

- #c3b9a0 (warm sand) - background, feels like old paper/concrete
- #4e736d (muted teal) - containers, like oxidized metal
- #e5a985 (peach pop) - accents, like a warm glow in the dark
- #494238 (brown dark) - depth and shadows
- #2a1b14 (brown deep) - text and grounding elements

Fonts:
- Laquer: Logo and game titles (hand-drawn, grungy feel)
- Montserrat: Headings and buttons (clean contrast to grunge)
- TT Fors: Body text (monospace, technical)

Interactive Elements:
- loafing cat spinner during checkout
- floating dust particles for atmosphere
- subtle CRT scanline effect
- pawprints of cats patrolling through the space
- ambient background music

Inspiration:
